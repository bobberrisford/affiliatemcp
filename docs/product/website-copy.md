# Website copy deck

> Status: working draft. Companion to [`website-plan.md`](./website-plan.md).
> Voice: plain, honest, confident. UK English ("programme"). Jargon
> (MCP, adapter, stdio) stays off user-facing pages.
>
> **Positioning:** the end game is not a chatbot for dashboards — it is
> affiliate work that runs itself, with the user deciding what "itself" is
> allowed to do. The site sells the destination (automation) and the safe path
> to it (the five-rung ladder), while staying honest about what is available
> today vs. on the roadmap.

**Sub-line:** Pulling reports, chasing pending commissions, checking links,

### The shift, in one line
Your affiliate data is scattered across dashboards, exports, and spreadsheets —
and *doing* anything with it is all you. We bring it into the AI you already
use, then take the routine work off your plate, one rung at a time.

### The ladder (centrepiece)
Through-line: **See it → trust it → let it run.** Each step carries a subtle
availability marker so we sell the vision without over-promising.

1. **Connect** · `Available now`
   Your network data, in Claude and Codex. Your logins stay on your machine.
2. **Analyse** · `Available now`
   Ask anything, across every network and brand at once. Earnings, pending,
   reversals, dead links, anomalies — in plain English.
3. **Strategy & KPIs** · `Coming`
   Say what you're trying to achieve. It tracks where you stand against the
   targets that matter — not just what happened.
4. **Approvals** · `Coming`
   Decide what you sign off and what you delegate. A safe system for what runs
   without you and what always waits for your yes.
5. **Automation** · `Coming`
   The routine work runs itself — the doing layer acts within your rules and
   brings everything else to you.

### Why you can let it run (trust)
- **Your data, your machine.** Logins live on your computer, not a hosted
  account. Networks see the same calls as your own dashboard.
- **You set the limits.** Automation only ever acts within rules you approve.
  Nothing runs behind your back.
- **No telemetry.** Nothing phones home. No tracking, no account.
- **Free and open source.** MIT licensed — read every line, or contribute your
  own.

### Catches what dashboards bury
The questions you'd never think to click for, surfaced automatically:
transactions stuck pending, dead links across your whole site, reversal spikes,
week-on-week drops, programmes drifting toward zero. Ask once — or, soon, let it
watch for you.

## Tagline system (three lines, by role)

The exploration produced three strong lines. Rather than pick one, each owns the
spot where it works hardest:

| Line | Role |
| --- | --- |
| **Agentic affiliate** | One-word category tagline — logo lockup, meta/OG title (e.g. "Agentic affiliate · take the grind out of affiliate marketing"), the one-sentence description of what this is |
| **Affiliate AI that actually does things** | Lead-in above the homepage ladder — the bridge from the hero to the five rungs |
| **Automating affiliate** | `/mission` page H1 — the plain statement of the end game where the full vision is told |

The homepage hero itself is the grind-led line above ("Take the grind out of
affiliate marketing"), because a verb that promises relief pulls harder on the
non-technical reader than a descriptor of the product.

## Notes & rejected directions

Hero directions considered (kept for reference):
- *Doing layer:* "The doing layer for affiliate." Strong, but "layer" is faintly
  technical; promoted "Agentic affiliate" to the tagline slot instead.
- *Vision + control:* "Affiliate work that runs itself — with you in control."
- *The arc:* "From 'show me the numbers' to 'handle it.'"
- Earlier, pre-automation framing: "Chat with your affiliate data — in the AI
  you already use." Superseded once the end game was clarified as automation.

The `72 networks` figure and network counts must be sourced from the same data
the README auto-generates (see `website-plan.md` §8), never hand-typed.
